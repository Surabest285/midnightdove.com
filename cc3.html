Module 4: Offensive Tactics

  • Hand-to-hand combat techniques
  • Tactical maneuvers and team coordination
  • Navigation skills and techniques
  • Camouflage and concealment strategies


    • Course Overview:
    The Offensive Tactics course is designed to provide participants with a comprehensive understanding of offensive operations in a military context. Participants will learn the fundamental principles, strategies, and techniques used in offensive tactics to seize and maintain the initiative against an adversary. The course covers a range of topics, including offensive mission planning, maneuver warfare, coordination and synchronization of forces, and the application of firepower. Through practical exercises and simulations, participants will develop the skills and knowledge necessary to plan and execute successful offensive operations.
    Course Duration: 1 week
    Course Outline:
    1. Introduction to Offensive Tactics - Importance and objectives of offensive operations - Historical examples and case studies of successful offensive campaigns - Overview of the course objectives and structure
    2. Offensive Mission Planning - Conducting a mission analysis for offensive operations - Defining objectives and determining the desired end-state - Formulating a clear and achievable mission statement - Identifying key tasks and sequencing of actions
    3. Offensive Strategies and Principles - Understanding offensive strategies, such as frontal assault, flanking maneuvers, and envelopment - Principles of offensive operations, including surprise, concentration of force, and exploitation - Application of maneuver warfare concepts in offensive tactics - Balancing aggression with operational security
    4. Firepower and Fire Support - Integration of firepower in offensive operations - Selection and coordination of direct and indirect fire assets - Close air support and artillery support planning - Maximizing the effectiveness of supporting fires
    5. Offensive Maneuver and Movement - Movement techniques for offensive operations - Flanking maneuvers, infiltration, and penetration tactics - Securing objectives and consolidating gains - Exploiting success and maintaining momentum
    6. Synchronization and Coordination - Coordination and synchronization of multiple units in offensive operations - Integration of combined arms assets (infantry, armor, artillery, etc.) - Liaison and communication between different elements - Timely and effective execution of supporting tasks
    7. Offensive Combat Support - Integration of combat support elements (engineers, logistics, medical, etc.) - Mobility and countermobility operations - Route reconnaissance and clearance - Resupply and casualty evacuation planning
    8. Offensive Countermeasures - Dealing with enemy defensive measures and obstacles - Techniques for breaching enemy defenses - Reacting to enemy counterattacks and flanking attempts - Neutralizing enemy firepower and defenses
    9. Urban Offensive Operations - Tactics for conducting offensive operations in urban environments - Close quarters battle and room clearing techniques - Coordinating with supporting elements in built-up areas - Minimizing civilian casualties and collateral damage
    10. Night Offensive Operations - Planning and executing offensive operations during hours of limited visibility - Special considerations for night attacks and maneuvers - Employment of night vision equipment and tactics - Maintaining command and control in low-light conditions
    11. After Action Review and Lessons Learned - Conducting post-mission analysis and assessment - Extracting lessons learned for continuous improvement - Documentation and dissemination of best practices - Importance of debriefing and incorporating feedback
    12. Conclusion and Practical Application - Recap of key concepts and principles covered in the course - Practical application exercises and simulations - Emphasis on the importance of continuous training and refinement of offensive tactics skills
    Note: The Offensive Tactics course provides participants with a solid foundation in offensive operations. It is recommended that participants continue their training and gain real-world experience to further develop their offensive tactics expertise.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the purpose and significance of Tactical Operations in the military.
  • Recognize the physical fitness requirements and conditioning exercises in Tactical Operations.
  • Gain knowledge of weapons training, including firearm safety, marksmanship, and tactical shooting.
  • Develop basic combat skills, such as hand-to-hand combat, tactical maneuvers, navigation, and camouflage.
  • Comprehend the importance of military discipline, customs, and courtesies.
  • Build physical and mental toughness, including endurance, stress management, confidence, and teamwork.
  • Understand and apply the core values and ethics of the military.
  • Prepare for personal readiness in various military situations, including first aid, survival skills, and hygiene.
  • Recognize the requirements for completing Tactical Operations and understand the next steps in military career progression.

Course Duration

This course on Tactical Operations is self-paced and can be completed in approximately 4 weeks, depending on your learning speed and dedication. Each module consists of multiple lessons, and it is recommended to spend at least 2-3 hours per week on the course materials and exercises.

Assessment and Certification

To successfully complete the course and earn a certificate of completion, you will need to complete all modules and pass the final assessment. The assessment will test your understanding of the key concepts and skills covered in the course. Upon passing the assessment, you will receive a certificate recognizing your achievement in completing the Tactical Operations Course.

Prerequisites

There are no prerequisites for this course. It is open to anyone interested in learning about Basic Combat Training in the military.

To begin the course, click on Module 1: Introduction to Tactical Operations in the course syllabus above. Each module contains lessons and resources to guide you through the content. Take your time to read the materials, watch any provided videos, and complete the exercises to reinforce your learning.
